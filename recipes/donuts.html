What Are Donuts?

Donuts (or doughnuts) are sweet, fluffy, fried pastries enjoyed all around the world. They can be glazed, filled, powdered, or topped with chocolate and sprinkles. This recipe follows a classic yeast-raised donut style—soft, airy, and perfect for glazing.

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 cup warm milk
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • Oil for frying

Glaze Ingredients

  • 2 cups powdered sugar
  • ¼ cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

How to Make It (Steps)

  1. Warm the milk until slightly hot to the touch and mix in the yeast. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar, eggs, melted butter, and vanilla.
  3. Add in the yeast mixture and stir until combined.
  4. Slowly add the flour and salt, mixing until a soft dough forms.
  5. Knead the dough for 5–7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  6. Cover the bowl and let the dough rise for 1–1.5 hours, or until doubled in size.
  7. Roll out the dough to about ½-inch thickness and cut into donut shapes.
  8. Let the cut donuts rest for another 20–30 minutes to rise.
  9. Heat oil in a deep pot to 350°F (175°C).
  10. Fry each donut for 1–2 minutes per side until golden brown.
  11. Place fried donuts on paper towels to drain excess oil.
  12. For the glaze, whisk together powdered sugar, milk, and vanilla.
  13. Dip each warm donut into the glaze and set on a rack to drip.
  14. Serve warm and enjoy your fresh homemade donuts!
